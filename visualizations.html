    The choropleth graph is a visualization that shows the distribution of TV show production around the world, having a particular focus on Netflix original shows. The graph shows that the US is the leading producer of Netflix original shows, followed by countries like Japan, Korea, and Canada. The darker colors on the graph represent a higher number of shows produced in that region. Using the visualization, the analysis suggests that the US will remain a powerhouse in TV show production, but the growth and prominence of international TV shows are expected to increase in the coming years. An example is the rise of Korean and Japanese dramas, this implies that other countries like India or New Zealand may also produce a significant number of unique TV shows. This idea aligns with the trend of diversification in the streaming industry, where more people are seeking content from various regions and cultures apart from their own.
